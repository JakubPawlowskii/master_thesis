\vspace*{6cm}
\abstract{\noindent
Slowing down thermalization in isolated quantum systems is one of the most promising
problems in modern condensed matter physics, with a wide range of immediate applications.
A possible path to achieve this goal is to consider integrable systems, in which thermalization
is hindered by the existence of an extensive number of local integrals of motion.
Unfortunately, the integrability of quantum systems is often a fragile phenomenon and relies on a fine-tuned
set of parameters. More realistic systems are therefore expected to be
\textit{nearly integrable}, i.e. contain some small integrability-breaking perturbation.
In this work, we investigate spin transport in the long-range anisotropic Heisenberg model,
with power-law decaying interactions \(J(\alpha) = J/r^{\alpha }\), and show that it can be
considered a nearly integrable system.
We arrive at this conclusion based on the analysis of spin domain expansion (short times)
and the linear response theory of optical conductivity (long times), which both indicate 
the presence of quasiballistic spin transport along an optimal anisotropy line \(\Delta \approx \exp(-\alpha + 2)  \)
in the parameter space. Moreover, using an algorithm detecting most conserved operators,
we demonstrate that this behavior is not unique to the nonlocal spin current,
but also exhibited by a family of slowly relaxing, local fermionic currents. All novel results
presented in this thesis were first published in a paper by~\textcite{Mierzejewski2023}.
}
\begin{quotation}
	\noindent \textbf{Keywords:} \textit{Eigenstate Thermalization Hypothesis, spin transport, local slowly relaxing operators, nearly integrable systems, spin chains}
\end{quotation}
